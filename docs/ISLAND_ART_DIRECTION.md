# Island Art Direction

Last accepted: 2026-07-16

This is the accepted visual-identity contract for all 41 floating islands. “Hero quality” here means
that every island has an individually authored identity, readable landmark, ecology, geology,
history, palette, and water story within NAU's deterministic prototype-art style. It does not mean
production asset fidelity.

`IslandComposition` remains authoritative for traversal purpose, route relationships, altitude,
and player-facing navigation. `IslandArtDirection` is authoritative for visual identity:

- epithet and environmental story
- palette family and surface pattern
- one island-specific hero landmark
- exact flora, formation, and ruin inventories
- water story and feature presence
- deterministic review framing

## Hard Acceptance Contract

- Exactly 41 ordered profiles must match the 41 route islands.
- Each full art-direction signature must match its accepted baseline, and art-direction
  signatures, palette tuples, and complete aggregate visual signatures must all be unique. The
  signature includes every authored story, palette adjustment, pattern, landmark transform,
  feature inventory/anchor, water story, review heading, and seed.
- Every island must export one ground-cover entry, its exact accepted tree/rock counts, and exactly
  one matching hero landmark. The accepted route totals 171 trees and 282 rocks; reducing a rich
  island to a generic one-tree/one-rock minimum fails the audit.
- Each profile's exact flora, formation, and ruin inventory must be realized. The accepted route
  contains 102 flora clusters, 69 geological formations, and 46 ruin complexes.
- Profile budgets are 2–3 flora clusters, 1–2 formations, and 0–2 ruin complexes per island.
- Water visuals must exist exactly for the 16 non-dry stories and remain absent from the 25
  dry/wind-carved stories.
- Islands with at least 5,000 m² of ground-cover footprint must retain at least 20% authored-feature
  footprint coverage. The accepted set's lowest large-island ratio is 23.4%.
- Every island must pass near, mid, and traversal review, for exactly 123 screenshot and semantic
  checkpoints.
- Hero-landmark evidence uses multiple deterministic sample points derived from real generated mesh
  triangles, not a single coarse bounding-box point. Semantic island occlusion uses the authored
  visual footprint rather than a generic ellipse.
- Review framing and rendered route-edge waterfalls share one placement contract. Non-plateau
  waterfall-garden near views must keep the rendered ribbon and mist inside a 70% normalized
  safe-frame bound.
- Manual review remains required after automated visual and semantic gates. The accepted 123-image
  set was inspected for composition, silhouette, lighting, feature readability, repetition, route
  clearance, and obvious collision or streaming artifacts.

## Accepted Evidence

The accepted visual-content export contains 1,347 meshes, 991,095 vertices, and 947,092 triangles,
including 41 ground-cover meshes, 171 trees, 282 rocks, 578 landmark entries, 102 flora clusters,
46 ruin complexes, 69 geological formations, and 56 water-detail clusters. All 41 terrain,
foliage, and stone palette tuples are distinct.

The accepted isolated play-performance run records:

- debug: 14.65 ms average, 17.52 ms p95, zero frames over 33.34 ms
- release: 16.18 ms average, 17.37 ms p95, zero frames over 33.34 ms
- debug/release average ratio: 0.905

## Required Regression Workflow

```sh
cargo fmt --check
cargo check --all-targets
cargo clippy --all-targets --all-features -- -D warnings
cargo test --all-targets
./tools/island_art_direction_gate.sh target/eval/island_art_direction
./tools/world_content_gate.sh target/eval/world_content
./tools/camera_continuity_gate.sh target/eval/camera_continuity
./tools/dev_play_performance_gate.sh target/eval/dev_play_performance
```

`world_content_gate.sh` includes the 41-island × 3-view hero gallery. The gallery gate requires the
review manifest, all 123 PNGs, all 123 marker sidecars, visual-audit success, semantic-scene success,
and exact near/mid/traversal coverage. CI uploads any gallery evidence produced before a failed
world gate exits. The accepted run passed 155 runtime checks, 18 semantic checks, and 16 visual
checks across all 123 checkpoints with a 0.0000 m maximum grounded visual foot gap. The independent
camera-continuity matrix passed all 13 scenarios.

Automated success does not waive direct image inspection. Any change to island placement, palette,
terrain, feature generation, lighting, camera review framing, LOD/streaming, or collision must
review the affected captures and rerun the complete gate chain.
